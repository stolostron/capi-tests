# English Vocabulary Guide - AI Action Words

This guide explains the vocabulary commonly used by Claude Code when describing actions, particularly during code analysis, exploration, and development tasks. Words are categorized by formality level and usage context, with Czech language equivalents provided.

## Category 1: FORMAL / TECHNICAL / ACADEMIC

### **Inferring**
- **Meaning**: Drawing logical conclusions from evidence or reasoning; deducing
- **Usage**: Formal, technical, academic, AI/ML contexts
- **Czech**: odvozovat, usuzovat, vyvozovat
- **Example**: "Inferring the error type from the stack trace"

### **Manifesting**
- **Meaning**: Making something visible or evident; displaying clearly; bringing into reality
- **Usage**: Formal, philosophical, technical (can also be spiritual)
- **Czech**: projevovat se, zobrazovat se, zhmotňovat
- **Example**: "The bug is manifesting in production environments"

### **Envisioning**
- **Meaning**: Imagining or visualizing a future possibility; forming a mental image
- **Usage**: Formal, business, design, strategic planning
- **Czech**: představovat si, vizualizovat, zamýšlet
- **Example**: "Envisioning the final architecture of the system"

### **Pondering**
- **Meaning**: Thinking about something carefully and deeply; considering thoughtfully
- **Usage**: Formal/neutral, literary, academic
- **Czech**: uvažovat, přemýšlet, rozjímat
- **Example**: "Pondering the best approach to solve this problem"

## Category 2: INFORMAL / COLLOQUIAL

### **Spelunking**
- **Meaning**: Literally "cave exploring"; metaphorically "diving deep into code/data"
- **Usage**: Informal, technical slang, programming culture
- **Czech**: (doslova) jeskyňářství; (přeneseně) procházení/zkoumání (kódu)
- **Example**: "Spelunking through the legacy codebase to find the bug"

### **Sussing** (out)
- **Meaning**: Figuring out, investigating, discovering; understanding something unclear
- **Usage**: Informal, British English origin, colloquial
- **Czech**: zjišťovat, pátrat, přicházet na
- **Example**: "Sussing out what went wrong with the deployment"

### **Finagling**
- **Meaning**: Obtaining by clever or devious means; working around obstacles creatively
- **Usage**: Informal, slightly playful, can imply cleverness or trickery
- **Czech**: vymýšlet si, vyhrávat, vykutálit (něco)
- **Example**: "Finagling a workaround for the API limitation"

### **Hatching**
- **Meaning**: Devising or formulating (a plan); creating something new
- **Usage**: Informal, metaphorical (from hatching eggs)
- **Czech**: vymýšlet, kout (plány), kuchtit
- **Example**: "Hatching a plan to refactor the authentication system"

### **Smoshing** (Smooshing/Squishing)
- **Meaning**: Combining or merging things together; compressing or flattening
- **Usage**: Very informal, playful, onomatopoetic
- **Czech**: mačkat dohromady, slučovat, cpát
- **Example**: "Smoshing these two functions into one"
- **Note**: Not in standard dictionaries; internet/casual slang

## Category 3: NEUTRAL / DESCRIPTIVE

### **Wandering**
- **Meaning**: Moving aimlessly or exploratively; browsing without specific direction
- **Usage**: Neutral, descriptive, can be literal or metaphorical
- **Czech**: bloudit, toulat se, procházet se
- **Example**: "Wandering through the file structure to understand the project"

### **Puzzling**
- **Meaning**: Being confused or perplexed; trying to solve something difficult
- **Usage**: Neutral, can be verb or adjective
- **Czech**: lámání hlavy, přemýšlení (nad), být zmatený
- **Example**: "Puzzling over why the tests are failing"

### **Unfurling**
- **Meaning**: Unfolding or opening up gradually; revealing progressively
- **Usage**: Neutral, poetic/literary (from unfurling sails or flags)
- **Czech**: rozvíjet se, rozvinovat se, odhalovat se
- **Example**: "The project structure is unfurling as I explore the repository"

## Additional AI/Programming Action Words

### FORMAL / TECHNICAL

**Analyzing**
- **Meaning**: Examining in detail to understand structure or meaning
- **Czech**: analyzovat, rozbírat
- **Usage**: Very formal, technical, academic

**Evaluating**
- **Meaning**: Assessing value, quality, or significance
- **Czech**: vyhodnocovat, posuzovat, hodnotit
- **Usage**: Formal, business, academic

**Synthesizing**
- **Meaning**: Combining elements to form a coherent whole
- **Czech**: syntetizovat, slučovat, spojovat
- **Usage**: Formal, academic, technical

**Scrutinizing**
- **Meaning**: Examining very carefully and critically
- **Czech**: zkoumat důkladně, pátrat, přezkoumávat
- **Usage**: Formal, can imply skepticism

**Refining**
- **Meaning**: Improving by making small changes; polishing
- **Czech**: zpřesňovat, vylepšovat, kultivovat
- **Usage**: Formal/neutral, technical

**Orchestrating**
- **Meaning**: Arranging or coordinating complex activities
- **Czech**: orchestrovat, řídit, koordinovat
- **Usage**: Formal, business, technical

---

### INFORMAL / COLLOQUIAL

**Digging** (into/through)
- **Meaning**: Investigating deeply; searching thoroughly
- **Czech**: rýt (do), hrabat se (v), prohrabávat se
- **Usage**: Informal, common in tech culture

**Wrangling**
- **Meaning**: Managing or controlling something difficult; dealing with complexity
- **Czech**: potýkat se (s), řešit (problém), ovládat
- **Usage**: Informal, American English, common in "data wrangling"

**Tinkering**
- **Meaning**: Making small adjustments or experiments; playing with
- **Czech**: kutit, experimentovat, vylepšovat
- **Usage**: Informal, implies playful experimentation

**Poking** (around/at)
- **Meaning**: Examining casually; testing or prodding
- **Czech**: šťourat (v), zkoušet, prozkoumávat
- **Usage**: Very informal, casual

**Munching** (through data)
- **Meaning**: Processing large amounts of data
- **Czech**: zpracovávat (data), "žvýkat" data
- **Usage**: Very informal, playful

**Brewing**
- **Meaning**: Developing or preparing something (like brewing coffee)
- **Czech**: chystat, připravovat, vařit (plán)
- **Usage**: Informal, metaphorical

**Juggling**
- **Meaning**: Managing multiple tasks or elements simultaneously
- **Czech**: žonglovat (s), zvládat současně
- **Usage**: Informal, common in work contexts

**Crafting**
- **Meaning**: Creating something with skill and care
- **Czech**: vytvářet, řemeslně zpracovávat
- **Usage**: Neutral/informal, implies artistry

---

### NEUTRAL / DESCRIPTIVE

**Exploring**
- **Meaning**: Investigating or examining to learn about
- **Czech**: zkoumat, prozkumávat, objevovat
- **Usage**: Neutral, common in all contexts

**Investigating**
- **Meaning**: Conducting systematic inquiry to discover facts
- **Czech**: vyšetřovat, zkoumat, pátrat
- **Usage**: Neutral/formal, official tone

**Traversing**
- **Meaning**: Moving through or across; navigating
- **Czech**: procházet, překonávat, proplouvat
- **Usage**: Neutral/technical, common in algorithms

**Parsing**
- **Meaning**: Analyzing structure (especially text/code); breaking down into components
- **Czech**: parsovat, analyzovat, rozkládat
- **Usage**: Technical, programming specific

**Examining**
- **Meaning**: Inspecting closely and carefully
- **Czech**: zkoumat, prohlížet, vyšetřovat
- **Usage**: Neutral, formal

**Navigating**
- **Meaning**: Finding one's way through; managing course through complexity
- **Czech**: navigovat, orientovat se, procházet
- **Usage**: Neutral, technical

**Scanning**
- **Meaning**: Looking through quickly; searching systematically
- **Czech**: skenovat, prohledávat, prohlížet
- **Usage**: Neutral, technical

## Notes on Usage

**Informal/colloquial words** make AI communication more natural and human-like, creating a conversational tone that's easier to follow during complex technical work.

**Formal words** maintain professionalism in technical contexts and are appropriate for documentation, reports, and formal communications.

**Context-specific words** help convey precise meaning within specialized domains (programming, business, academia) and demonstrate domain expertise.

This vocabulary reflects Claude Code's approach to making technical AI assistance feel more approachable while maintaining precision and clarity.
